**Weekdays:**

* **6:00-7:00 AM:  "Me Time"** Wake up before the rest of the family.  Use this time for exercise, meditation, journaling, or simply enjoying a cup of coffee in peace.
* **7:00-8:00 AM: Family Time**  Wake up kids, breakfast together, pack lunches, get everyone ready for the day.
* **8:00-9:00 AM:  Work/Family Transition**  Drop kids at school/daycare, commute, settle into your workspace. If working from home, this could involve quick household chores.
* **9:00 AM-12:00 PM: Focused Work Block**  Minimize distractions. Tackle your most demanding tasks during this period.
* **12:00-1:00 PM:  Lunch & Break**  Step away from work. Enjoy a healthy lunch.  Read, take a walk, or do something relaxing.
* **1:00-4:00 PM: Work Block 2**  Continue with work tasks, meetings, etc.
* **4:00-5:00 PM: Work Wrap-up/Family Transition**  Finish up urgent tasks, plan for tomorrow, pick up kids from school/daycare.
* **5:00-6:00 PM: Family Time**  Connect with your family. Prepare dinner together, chat about their day.
* **6:00-7:00 PM: Dinner Time**  Eat together as a family.  No phones at the table!
* **7:00-8:00 PM: Family Time/Kids' Bedtime Routine**  Bath time, story time, get kids ready for bed.
* **8:00-9:00 PM:  Family Time/Wind Down**  Tidy up, spend time with your partner, read.
* **9:00-10:00 PM:  "Me Time"**  A short period for relaxation before bed. Take a bath, read, meditate.
* **10:00 PM:  Bedtime**  Prioritize sleep!

**Weekends:**

* Aim for a less structured schedule but still incorporate designated family time, self-care, and some catch-up time for work or household chores.
* Schedule specific "Me Time" activities - a long bath, a massage, a walk with a friend, a hobby.
* Plan family outings and activities.
* Include a block of time for meal prepping or planning for the week ahead.  This will save time and reduce stress during the week.


**Tips for Success:**

* **Be Flexible:**  Things will come up. Adjust your schedule as needed.
* **Prioritize:**  Focus on the most important tasks first.
* **Delegate:**  Don't try to do everything yourself. Involve your partner, kids, and even consider outsourcing some tasks if possible.
* **Say No:**  It's okay to decline requests that don't align with your priorities.
* **Batch Similar Tasks:**  Group similar activities together to improve efficiency. For example, do all your errands on one day.
* **Schedule Self-Care:** Treat it like any other important appointment.
* **Unplug Regularly:**  Disconnect from technology to recharge and be present with your family.
* **Be Kind to Yourself:**  You're doing the best you can. Don't strive for perfection.


Remember, finding the right balance is an ongoing process.  Experiment with different schedules and find what works best for you and your family.
