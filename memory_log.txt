User: What is AI?
Agent: AI, or Artificial Intelligence, is a branch of computer science aiming to create machines capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. These tasks can include:

* **Learning:** Acquiring knowledge and skills from experience, data, or instructions.  This can involve various methods like machine learning (where algorithms identify patterns in data) and deep learning (using artificial neural networks with multiple layers).
* **Reasoning:**  Using rules and logic to reach conclusions and make decisions.
* **Problem-solving:**  Finding solutions to complex situations and challenges.
* **Perception:** Interpreting sensory information, such as images, sounds, and touch.  This allows AI systems to "see," "hear," and interact with the physical world.
* **Natural Language Processing (NLP):**  Understanding and generating human language, enabling AI to communicate with us in a way we understand.
* **Planning:** Setting goals and devising strategies to achieve them.


**Key aspects of AI:**

* **Data-driven:** Many modern AI systems rely heavily on data to learn and improve.  The more data they are trained on, the better they typically perform.
* **Algorithmic:** AI systems are based on algorithms, which are sets of instructions that tell the computer what to do.
* **Iterative:** AI development is an ongoing process of refinement and improvement.  Models are constantly being trained and updated to perform better.


**Types of AI:**

AI can be broadly categorized into different types based on their capabilities:

* **Narrow or Weak AI:**  Designed for a specific task, like playing chess or recommending products.  This is the most common type of AI currently in use.  Examples include Siri, Alexa, and spam filters.
* **General or Strong AI:**  Hypothetical AI with human-level intelligence capable of performing any intellectual task a human can.  This type of AI does not yet exist.
* **Super AI:**  Hypothetical AI that surpasses human intelligence in all aspects. This is purely theoretical at this point.



It's important to note that AI is a rapidly evolving field, and the definition and capabilities of AI are constantly expanding.  While the current focus is largely on narrow AI, the long-term goal for many researchers is to develop more general and adaptable AI systems.
